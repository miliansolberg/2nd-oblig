Is Nicotine the new "Limitless" drug?

Recent studies show nicotine's ability to promote neuroplasticity, and thereby improving learning.

Jack Kennedy 9/26/2023, 2:25 AM
A white Zyn nicotine pouch is displayed, with a bold "ZYN" logo, highlighting its smokeless nature.

Nicotine, the primary psychoactive substance in tobacco, has been shown to have a significant influence on the brain, and there is evidence suggesting it may increase neuroplasticity. Neuroplasticity refers to the brain's ability to reorganize and form new neural connections throughout life. Some studies indicate that nicotine can enhance synaptic plasticity and increase the release of neurotransmitters, which are critical for learning and memory. This might be attributed to the activation of nicotinic acetylcholine receptors, which play crucial roles in cognitive functions.
